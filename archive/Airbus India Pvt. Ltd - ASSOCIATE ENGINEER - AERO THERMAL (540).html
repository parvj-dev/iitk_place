Job description –Associate Engineer Non Linear Finite Element Analysis
Team: Airframe – Stress and Thermal Solutions
The Numerical Simulation and DFEM team is a part of the ‘Airframe Stress and Design’ group
under Airframe department and plays a key role providing solutions through assessment of
structural integrity of aircraft structures/assemblies.
The team extends competencies in area of Thermal and Internal Flows analysis using FE and
CFD based methods. Internal flows activities mainly deals with Ventilation of various A/C
bays, flows inside Ducts, cooling technologies, transport of various species etc and Thermal
Analysis to assess thermal impact on structures.
Role: To support aircraft Aerothermal/Internal Flows/Structural studies
The activities include:
 Understanding of Aircraft structure and systems
 Basics of fluid mechanics, theories and CFD
 Understanding of Heat Transfer process, Thermodynamics and material properties.
 Understanding of temperature mapping, effect and theories.
 Basics of Mathematical Modelling and Simulation techniques
 Structural Thermal Analysis to assess thermal impact on aircraft structures.
Technical skills required:
 Knowledge of Ansys Fluent, Star CCM+, Ideas TMG, NX-Thermal, Ansys MechanicalThermal is desirable.
 Exposure to Scripting (PYTHON), FE Solvers is preferable.
 Finite element method / finite volume analysis.
 Understanding of Heat Transfer process, Thermodynamics.
 Engineering Mechanics of composite/metallic Materials.
Tools: Ansa, Ansys Fluent, StarCCM+, OpenFoam, NX Thermal, Hypermesh
Programming language: Basics of programming -Python, Fortran etc
Soft Skills:
 Teamwork
 Excellent communication skills
 Willingness to learn new skills, self-development
 Problem-solving orientation
